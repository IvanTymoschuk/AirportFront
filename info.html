History

In the summer of 1945, a flight to the former military airfield was opened for flights of aircraft of the PO-2 airborne units of the GVF, based in Lviv. This unit was supposed to maintain contact with the northern regions of the Rivne region, to provide them with newspapers, to transport mail, and so on. First flight to Rivne-Antalya on 07/10/2016 In 1950, the air division increased the number of vessels, it was converted to 224 aircraft units of the Ukrainian Civil Aviation Fleet (CFA). In 1955, it was reorganized into the 94th aviation branch of the GPP. 1962 - a new reorganization, in which three airplanes 94, 88 and 98 were combined into 91 aviation units of UTU TSFF, the air squadrons of which were based in Rivne, Lutsk and Ternopil. In the same year a new terminal was built. 1963 - The 91st Aircraft was reorganized into the "Rivne Combined Aircraft". At the end of the 1970s, the development of aviation in the Rivne region accelerated. In 1977, a modern air terminal with a high-quality runway was built in Rivne, which allowed the use of the Tu-134, Tu-154, Yak-40, Yak-42, Il-76 and others. In the early 80's a new type of aircraft is being developed - the L-410 UVP. 1990-1994 - search for ways to create a national civil aviation in Ukraine. United Airplanes, as large industrial structures decided to restructure again. In 1992, the airport received, and in 2005, confirmed the status of an international one. 1993 - on the basis of the Rivne Airborne Division, the State International Airport "Rivne" and the city regional branch of the airline ZANG were created. The ERTZ database and air traffic agency are separate. December 1994 - the airport and the Rivne branch of the ZANG are merged and created by the Rivne State Airline "Universal-Avia". On October 1, 2001, the All-Avia RDPA was reorganized by separating it from the State Enterprise "International Airport Rivne".

Current state

By 2016, the airport brings together 16 services and departments serving passengers, freight transport, charter flights, cafes, road freight transport, wholesale fuel trade. The company employs more than 100 employees. Hall of the airport "Rivne" On July 10, 2016, for the first time in the last 10 years, a passenger flight on the route Rivne-Antalya was performed. (Excluding charter non-scheduled private aviation flights). Since then, Bravo Airways has been performing weekly Sunday flights on the specified route on both sides. The customer of the charter flights is the tour operator TPG. In 2016, the airport of Rivne received 6,997 passengers. In 2015, the air port served only 394 passengers. The growth of passenger traffic at the Rovno airport was made possible thanks to the launch of charter programs. In July 2016, the airline Bravo Airways began flights on the route to Rivne-Antalya. In October, the carrier launched charter flights to Hurghada and Sharm el-Sheikh. According to the preliminary agreement, investors are ready to invest $ 60 million in airport development and repay all its debts.

Description

Glisada Rivne (airport) over the Kyiv-Chop Autobahn The airport was created in the days of the USSR. It is located 8 km from the center of Rivne, practically within the city. Near the airport are motorways (in particular, E-40), which allows you to get to the train station, bus station and city center in a matter of minutes. The airport covers an area of ​​170 hectares. It has two runways: one long 2626 m and a width of 42 m, the other - 655 m. As of 2016, only the first of the bands is in working condition. At the airport there are: ticket offices for ticket sales, airline offices, customs control point, border post, transportation service, official delegations hall, airlift service. At the station square there are: a 24-hour paid car park guarded, a cafe. Near the airport is Sofia Hotel. The city also has the hotels "Mir", "Ukraine", "Khutir" and others.
